The following are the aim of the Organic Food Production

• Organic foods often have more beneficial nutrients, such as antioxidants, than their conventionally grown counterparts.

• Produce food while establishing an ecological balance to prevent soil fertility or pest problems.

• Organic products reduce public health risks to farm workers, their families, and consumers by minimizing their exposure to toxic and persistent chemicals on the farm and in food.

• To reduced risk of certain chronic diseases such as cardiovascular and neurodegenerative diseases and certain cancers.
